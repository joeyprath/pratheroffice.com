Essays

Forward Memory

Inheritance shows up in places no genetic map accounts for. My boys run the exact same way I do. The same stiff stride, same achilles that screams if you look at it wrong, same knee that files a complaint halfway up a hill. It’s not genetic so much as it’s choreography that somehow made it into the family tree.

Minds work the same way. We each carry a stash of private stories, absorbed beliefs, and unspoken rules. These myths run underneath everything, shaping our days with authority.

A posture practiced long enough becomes personality. Patterns repeat until they become character. The mind, like the body, doesn’t acknowledge time.

And every once in a while, you catch it in the act: exposed beliefs stepping out of the shadows.

On Becoming Unpredictable

Watching Ken Burns: The American Revolution. You kind of know what you’re in for with the PBS stamp. The soft, assured tone that treats the past like it was always waiting to be interpreted by the present.

Expression has become predictable. Art drifts toward uniformity. Prediction machines thrive here. The greats built careers on aberration.

Here’s to becoming unpredictable once again.
